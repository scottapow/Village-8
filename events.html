Bolshoi Ballet Live Fall - Winter 2015 - 2016 Schedule

Ticket Price: $25.00 (Only $20.00 for students, children and seniors)

Opening the sixth season of cinema events is one of the greatest romantic ballets, GISELLE performed live on Sunday, October 11. John Neumeier’s masterpiece, THE LADY OF THE CAMELLIAS -- which only recently entered the Bolshoi’s repertoire to incredible success, will be performed live on December 6 -- followed by THE NUTCRACKER (recorded) on December 20. For the very first time, North American audiences will be able to see Jean-Cristophe Maillot’s brand new production of Shakespeare’s comedy THE TAMING OF THE SHREW on January 24, 2016. Closing the season is a same day as live broadcast of Fadeyechev’s dazzling DON QUIXOTE on April 10, 2016.

National Theatre Live Hamlet Live w/ Benedict Cumberbatch

Thursday, October 15, 2015 at 2pm EST

Ticket Price: $25.00 (Only $20.00 for students, children and seniors)

Academy Award® nominee Benedict Cumberbatch (BBC’s Sherlock, The Imitation Game, Frankenstein at the National Theatre) takes on the title role of Shakespeare’s great tragedy.
Directed by Lyndsey Turner (Posh, Chimerica) and produced by Sonia Friedman Productions, National Theatre Live will broadcast this eagerly awaited production live to cinemas.
As a country arms itself for war, a family tears itself apart. Forced to avenge his father’s death but paralysed by the task ahead, Hamlet rages against the impossibility of his predicament, threatening both his sanity and the security of the state.

Louisville Exclusive Films and 2015 Louisville 48 Hour Film Project

Louisville Exclusive Films First-run independent, foreign, and art-house films to Louisville. New film openings each Friday

July 10th - 30th
Me and Earl and the Dying Girl

High schooler Greg, who spends most of his time making parodies of classic movies with his co-worker Earl, finds his outlook forever altered after befriending a classmate who has just been diagnosed with cancer.
Rated: PG-13

July 24th - 30th
Runoff

The beauty of the land cannot mask the brutality of a farm town. As harvest draws near, Betty confronts a terrifying new reality and will go to desperate lengths to save her family when they are threatened with being forced from their land.
Rated: R

July 31st - August 6th
The Stanford Prison Experiment

Twenty-four male students out of seventy-five were selected to take on randomly assigned roles of prisoners and guards in a mock prison situated in the basement of the Stanford psychology building.
Rated: R

August 28th - Sept. 3rd
Z for Zachariah

In the wake of a disaster that wipes out most of civilization, two men and a young woman find themselves in an emotionally charged love triangle as the last known survivors.
Rated: PG-13

2015 Louisville 48-hour Film Project August 2nd and August 3rd

Filmmakers are taking over Louisville and turning it into movie-making madness for 48 hours! Teams of filmmakers have a single weekend - this one - to write, shoot and edit their short films! All completed entries will be unveiled on the big screen, at our premiere screenings at Village 8 on August 2 and August 3.
